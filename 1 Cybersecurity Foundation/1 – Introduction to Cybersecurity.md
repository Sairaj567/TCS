# 1. What is Cybersecurity?

## Definition

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting **systems, networks, applications, and data** from:

- Unauthorized access
- Digital attacks
- Data theft
- Damage
- Service disruption

It protects digital infrastructure ranging from personal devices to critical national infrastructure.

---

# 2. Goals of Cybersecurity (CIA Triad)

The **CIA Triad** is the foundation of cybersecurity.

| Principle | Description | Example |
|------------|-------------|---------|
| **Confidentiality** | Prevent unauthorized access to data | Password-protected bank account |
| **Integrity** | Ensure data remains accurate and unmodified | Digital signatures |
| **Availability** | Ensure systems remain accessible when needed | Website uptime |

**Remember:** CIA = Confidentiality + Integrity + Availability

# 5. Common Cyber Threats

## Malware

Malicious software designed to damage systems.

Types:

### Virus
- Attaches to files
- Requires user action

### Worm
- Self-replicates
- Spreads automatically

### Trojan
- Appears legitimate
- Executes malicious actions

### Ransomware
- Encrypts files
- Demands payment for recovery

---

## Phishing

A social engineering attack that tricks users into:

- Revealing passwords
- Downloading malware
- Visiting fake websites

### Spear Phishing

Highly targeted phishing attack against a specific individual or organization.

---

## DoS & DDoS

### DoS (Denial of Service)

Single source floods a server.

### DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service)

Multiple compromised devices (botnet) flood a target.

Goal:
- Make services unavailable.

---

## Insider Threats

Threats originating from:

- Employees
- Contractors
- Business partners

Can be:

- Intentional
- Accidental

---

## Advanced Persistent Threat (APT)

Characteristics:

- Long-term attack
- Highly targeted
- Often state-sponsored
- Difficult to detect
- Focuses on espionage or sabotage

# 6. Key Cybersecurity Terminology

## Threat

Anything capable of causing harm.

Examples:

- Hacker
- Malware
- Insider

---

## Vulnerability

A weakness that can be exploited.

Examples:

- Weak passwords
- Unpatched software
- Misconfigured systems

---

## Risk

The likelihood and impact of a threat exploiting a vulnerability.

**Relationship:**

Threat → Exploits → Vulnerability → Creates → Risk

---

## Exploit

A technique or code used to take advantage of a vulnerability.

---

## Attack Surface

All possible entry points into a system.

Examples:

- Open ports
- APIs
- Web applications
- IoT devices
- Employees

Smaller attack surface = Better security.

---

# 7. Security Technologies

## Firewall

Filters incoming and outgoing network traffic according to predefined security rules.

---

## Antivirus

Detects, blocks, and removes malicious software.

---

## IDS

Intrusion Detection System

Purpose:

- Detect suspicious activity
- Generate alerts

---

## IPS

Intrusion Prevention System

Purpose:

- Detect attacks
- Automatically block attacks

---

# 8. Data Protection Concepts

## Encryption

Converts readable data (plaintext) into unreadable data (ciphertext).

Purpose:

- Confidentiality

---

## Hashing

Converts data into a fixed-length hash.

Purpose:

- Integrity verification

Examples:

- SHA-256
- SHA-512

---

## Digital Signature

Combines hashing and encryption.

Provides:

- Authentication
- Integrity
- Non-repudiation

---

# 9. Authentication vs Authorization

| Authentication | Authorization |
|----------------|---------------|
| Verifies identity | Determines permissions |
| Login | Access Control |
| "Who are you?" | "What can you access?" |

# Key Takeaways

- Cybersecurity protects digital assets from cyber threats.
- The CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability) is the foundation of information security.
- Malware, phishing, DDoS, insider threats, and APTs are major cyber threats.
- Understand the relationship between **Threat → Vulnerability → Risk**.
- Firewalls, antivirus, IDS, and IPS help defend systems.
- Encryption protects confidentiality, hashing ensures integrity, and digital signatures verify authenticity.
- Authentication verifies identity, while authorization controls access.
- Cybersecurity offers diverse and high-demand career opportunities.
